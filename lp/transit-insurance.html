Transit Insurance

Transit insurance, protects against loss or damage during transportation due to accidents, theft, fire, natural disasters, and unforeseen events. It is commonly used by businesses and individuals involved in transporting goods. This insurance provides financial protection by covering the value of goods and associated shipping costs. It helps maintain reputation and customer satisfaction by ensuring obligations can be fulfilled despite unexpected circumstances.

Types of Transit Insurance

Types of transit insurance policies available include:

  • Single Transit: Designed for occasional shipments, providing coverage for a single voyage until the goods reach their destination.
  • Customized Plan: Tailored policies based on goods type, transaction limitations, location limitations, mode of transportation, and specific requirements.
  • Overnight Vehicles' Insurance Policy: Covers goods stored overnight in the vehicle during transportation.
  • Open Policy: Offers coverage for multiple transits within a specified period, typically one year, suitable for businesses with frequent shipments.
  • Goods in Transit through Third-Party Carrier Cover: Protects goods when transported through a third-party carrier that may not provide sufficient coverage.
  • Goods in Transit Through Own Carrier Cover: Covers goods against damages when using a personal vehicle for transportation.
  • Cover for Multiple Vehicles: Provides coverage for goods transported via multiple vessels under a single insurance plan.

During the transportation of goods, various types of accidents can occur. There are several risks involved, including the overturning of vessels, theft, collisions, fires, and natural disasters like earthquakes and floods. To ensure the protection of your goods and commodities throughout the transit process, as well as during loading and unloading, it is advisable to purchase an online transit policy. This policy can be instantly issued for a single transit and remains valid for the entire journey from the source to the destination. Additionally, depending on the chosen coverage, it offers additional benefits such as protection against jettison, washing overboard, fire, explosion, and other specified risks.

Transit insurance offers a comprehensive policy that covers compensation for potential risks that may lead to damage to transported items. The common perils safeguarded by transport or transit insurance include:

  • Earthquakes.
  • Fire.
  • Explosion.
  • Lightning.
  • Any kind of man-made or natural calamities.
  • Collisions of the vessel carrying the goods, resulting in damage.
  • Overturning of the transport vehicle.
  • Derailment of the vessel.
  • Sinking of the vessel.
  • Risks during the loading and unloading of goods.
  • Risks during the packing and unpacking of goods.
  • Malicious damages.
  • Accidental damages.
  • Theft.
  • Impact damages.

An transit insurance policy is ideal for individuals or companies involved in the frequent transportation of goods. The following categories of people typically purchase a transit insurance policy:

  • Traders, including both importers and exporters.
  • Manufacturers.
  • Transporters.
  • Aggregators.
  • Individuals or entities seeking insurance coverage for the transportation of their goods from the origin to the destination
